Operational Flow

System Architecture: End-to-End

01

Identity Binding & Issuance

Following the 2025 Digital ID rollout by NIDA[cite: 23, 104], UniChain binds a user's verified identity to a Cardano wallet. Institutions (like UR or HEC) issue academic credentials as tamper-proof metadata on the blockchain[cite: 65, 79].

02

Immutable Storage via Blockfrost

Using Blockfrost.io, we interact with the Cardano ledger to store and retrieve metadata. This replaces manual, paper-based records that currently cause 57% of SACCOs to suffer financial losses due to mismanagement[cite: 27, 75].

03

Aiken-Powered Enforcement

For P2P loans—like the case of Hakiza who lost funds in November 2025 due to lack of proof [cite: 33, 76]—we use Aiken smart contracts. These contracts provide automated, immutable proof-of-agreement and enforcement[cite: 70, 82].

04

One-Click Verification

*Note: During current development, we use mock verification to demonstrate the final user experience.*

Employers and verifiers can instantly check a credential's authenticity. This solves the "7-day wait period" currently required by Irembo for manual equivalences[cite: 12, 73].

System FAQ

Where is our data stored?

Operational data is managed via Firestore for speed, while the "Source of Truth" (certificates/contracts) is anchored on the Cardano blockchain.

How does it help someone like Hakiza?

It creates a time-stamped contract between a borrower and lender. If the borrower refuses to pay, the lender has immutable proof of debt for legal authorities[cite: 83, 90].
